Week starting 18/08/2010

Quoting Steve:
"Most of the meeting focused on a discussion about how to manage "fault tolerance" in the general sense, with a particular focus on how we might be able to avoid redoing work after a failure [for now, think of this as a fail stop master failure]. The general idea was to have one or more write-ahead logs which captured the { completed, in-progress, and not-started } state of the world. At least one WAL would deal with tasks, i.e which tasks had been started, which completed/committed, etc. An unanswered question was whether or not this would be the only kind of information recorded, or whether some separate WAL (or sim) would hold information about the various bits of information held in storage blocks on various nodes in the cluster. In the former case (task log only), data items can be reconstructed by working out which tasks committed on which workers, and hence locating the data on their disks (subject to the worker in question being currently available). In the latter case, the task log would note the generation of items of a certain UUID, but a separate structure would record the mapping from UUID to a set of potential locations. The former one is simpler, but the latter gives more flexibility (certainly if we're trying for memoization/caching across jobs). We didn't come to a decision about which to use..."
